## **Legal and Compliance Considerations**

Even when emailing businesses in Nigeria, international spam laws often apply:

* **CAN-SPAM (USA):** Applies to any commercial email, including B2B. It requires honest headers, no misleading subject lines, and an easy unsubscribe link. “The CAN-SPAM Act… establishes requirements for commercial messages”.

* **GDPR (EU):** If you have any recipients in the EU, you need clear consent. Personal data use is tightly regulated, and recipients have rights over how you use their information.

* **CASL (Canada):** Requires consent for emails to Canadians. Violations can incur heavy fines ($1M for individuals, $10M for businesses).

* **Nigeria NDPR (2019):** Nigeria’s Data Protection Regulation sets standards for personal data usage, aligning with global privacy norms. B2B emails are less restricted, but it’s important to protect any personal data.

* **Best Practices:** Always use opt-in lists and include a clear opt-out in every email. The FTC emphasizes that commercial emails must let recipients easily stop future emails.

Always follow email laws. For example, the CAN-SPAM Act mandates that each email “gives recipients the right to have you stop emailing them”. Using verified, permission-based lists protects your brand and avoids costly penalties.
