## **Legal Considerations and Compliance**

Email marketing to Moroccan and international contacts must respect data laws. Morocco itself has no strict national spam law, but complying with global regulations is essential:

* **GDPR (EU):** If any contacts are EU residents, GDPR mandates explicit opt-in consent for marketing emails. In fact, EU rules require consent before sending marketing messages. European-style privacy laws also influence practices worldwide.

* **CAN-SPAM (USA):** The U.S. CAN-SPAM Act requires every commercial email to include a clear opt-out link and the sender’s valid physical address. Senders must honor unsubscribe requests within 10 business days.

* **CASL (Canada):** Canada’s CASL outright prohibits sending business emails without prior consent (express or implied). It also requires the sender’s identity and a functional unsubscribe option in each message. Violating CASL can lead to heavy fines.

* **UK/PECR:** UK regulations (PECR) echo these rules. You cannot email individuals without consent. However, you **can** email corporate addresses, though it’s best practice to maintain a “do-not-email” list of any companies that object. Even B2B emails must include unsubscribe links.

The bottom line: use a reputable provider that guarantees compliance. LeadsBlue and similar vendors typically ensure their Morocco email databases are built in accordance with GDPR, CAN-SPAM, and other privacy laws. That way, your campaigns stay lawful and you avoid penalties.
