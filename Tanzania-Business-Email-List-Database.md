## **Legal & Compliance Considerations**

Email campaigns must follow regulations like CASL, CAN-SPAM and GDPR. Ensuring compliance protects your brand and avoids fines:

* **GDPR (EU Privacy Law):** Treat Tanzanian email data as **personal data** if it includes individuals’ emails. GDPR requires **opt-in consent** before contacting EU citizens, provides data subject rights (e.g. deletion upon request), and mandates you document consent. Even if targeting non-EU audiences, GDPR-style opt-in practices improve trust.

* **CAN-SPAM (USA):** U.S. law mandates any commercial email have a clear **unsubscribe** link, accurate subject lines, and valid sender info. You don’t need prior permission to send, but you *must* honor opt-out requests and include your postal address. Violations can incur steep fines.

* **CASL (Canada):** Canada’s Anti-Spam Legislation is stricter: it requires **express consent (opt-in)** before emailing Canadians and demands easy unsubscribe options. CASL also covers all electronic marketing and imposes penalties up to millions.

* **Best Practices:** Reputable list providers gather contacts in compliance with these laws. LeadsBlue, for example, sources emails with lawful consent or legitimate interest. Always include unsubscribe links and sender ID in your messages. This ensures high deliverability and respects recipients.

In practice, compliance means: *only* email people who have agreed (or with whom you have a prior relationship), always include your business address and unsubscribe link, and promptly honor opt-outs. As one industry guide notes, CAN-SPAM and CASL focus on transparency and opt-out mechanisms, while GDPR emphasizes prior consent. By using a vetted Tanzania email database and following these rules, marketers maintain trust and avoid penalties.
